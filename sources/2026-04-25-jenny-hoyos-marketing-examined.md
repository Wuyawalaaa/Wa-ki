# Jenny Hoyos Short-Form Video Playbook (Marketing Examined)

**Original URL:** https://www.marketingexamined.com/blog/jenny-hoyos-short-form-video-playbook
**Platform:** Marketing Examined
**Fetched:** 2026-04-25

## The 3-second hook

- **Maximum: 3 seconds**, ideally less.
- Opening must be **concise and visually pleasing**.
- Power words that work: **"banned," "free," "one dollar," "secret," "cheap"**.
- Example hook: **"$1 chicken sandwich"**.
- Hook is so important Jenny will change the *entire video idea* if needed to make the hook strong.

## Retention structure

- **Target benchmark: 90% or higher retention rate.**
- The structure is: **hook → foreshadowing → transitions → but/then storytelling → emotional close**.

## Foreshadowing (post-hook)

Immediately after the hook, signal the conflict and expected outcome.

Example: *"Chick-fil-A has the best chicken sandwiches, but I am not paying $6..."*

The foreshadow does three jobs:
1. Signals what's coming
2. Surfaces the conflict
3. Implies the expected outcome (which the video may then twist)

## Transitions

Move the story forward fast. Example transition: *"Let's get cooking."*

## But/Then pattern

Used to set up turns and surprises in the narrative.

Example: *"But is that enough to beat Chick-fil-A?"*

## Closing strategy

End on **highest emotional intensity** — punchline, heartwarming moment, or twist. Goal: *"Leave them satisfied, but surprised."*

## Metrics

- 3 million YouTube subscribers
- Average **10 million views per YouTube Short**
