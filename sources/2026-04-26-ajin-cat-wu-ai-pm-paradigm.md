## Six takeaways

| # | Takeaway | Mechanic / claim |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Accelerated product cycles | Daily/weekly timelines replace 6–12 month planning. Use **"research preview"** labels to ship and test experimental features without commitment to long-term support. |
| 2 | Minimal documentation | PRDs are written only for: ambiguous projects, infrastructure investments, or failure-mode definitions. Weekly metrics + team principles replace most other documentation. |
| 3 | Decision-making is the bottleneck | "Code 越来越便宜，贵的是'决定写什么'" (code is increasingly cheap; deciding what to build is the scarce resource). PM hiring favors **engineers with product taste**. |
| 4 | "Just-right AGI" approach | Don't build for the future super-model — maximize the **current** model's capabilities. Example: Claude Code's todo list evolved from a memory aid (when models forgot context) into a progress display (as model capability improved). |
| 5 | Evaluation-driven development | Writing evals + asking models to **explain their failures** clarifies success metrics and isolates whether problems live in: prompting, tooling, processes, or user education. |
| 6 | Automation thresholds | **95% automation creates trust issues** (the 5% surprises break trust the other 95% built). Aim for near-100%, or avoid automation entirely. The middle is the worst place to land. |
