# 在X上成功launch了我第一个产品后的心得

**Author:** mori尧
**Title:** 在X上成功launch了我第一个产品后的心得 (Insights After Successfully Launching My First Product on X)

## Setup

The creator launched a product on X (formerly Twitter) **with two-digit followers initially**. Outcome over 3 days:

- **30k views**
- **200+ users**
- **1,043 messages**

## Four stated strategies

### 1. Precise targeting

Identify your desired audience segment **before** crafting your narrative. The creator targeted creatives interested in design, products, and AI.

### 2. Staged storytelling

Present your product from multiple angles rather than a single launch post. The creator's sequence:

> demo → formal launch → behind-the-scenes video → tutorial

### 3. Audience-centric messaging

Find the **intersection between what you want to share and what your target audience cares about**, rather than pushing what you want to say.

### 4. Video content

Post videos (screen recordings or talking-head format).

> **"X is full of nerds"** who rarely see coherent spoken-word content, providing a competitive advantage.

## Closing reflection

> In the AI era, **novelty and personal resonance** matter more than mere utility in software products.

## Notes for ingest

- Self-reported launch metrics from a single creator. Directional rather than benchmark-quality.

- The 4 strategies are the creator's own retrospective framing — they're a narrative-organized post, not a controlled experiment. Order may reflect storytelling, not causal weight.
