# Key extracted facts

## 1. Concentric Circles Framework

Buteau's summary (synthesis, not direct Lulu quote):

> "expanding outward in sequence: self → co-founders → employees → investors → power users"

> "gain clarity, then move to your co-founders, employees, investors, and power users, expanding outward in sequence"

No mechanism for inter-ring social proof is detailed in Buteau's synthesis. Original mechanism description lives in the Lenny Rachitsky podcast episode (timestamp 20:25–21:32).

## 2. Cultural Erogenous Zones

**Direct Lulu quote (via Buteau)**:

> "People have things that they either care about or don't and you're not going to change that. So it's a huge lift to try to change someone's worldview or their passions. It's a light lift to take the thing you want to talk about and just shape it into to fit into their worldview or their passions."

Buteau's framing: build "an API to your audience" by bridging what they already care about to your message.

## 3. Physics-Based Communications

Not covered in Buteau's synthesis.

## 4. Sticky Phrases / Memorable Framings

Buteau identifies three techniques:
- **Analogy** ("put the pill in some cheese")
- **Stories over adjectives** — anecdotes instead of subjective descriptors
- **ABBA structure for slogans** — referenced, not detailed

## 5. Go Direct

**Direct Lulu quote (via Buteau)**:

> "The crux of what it means is for the founder and the originator of the project to speak directly to the audience without middlemen, without screens, without filtering it through all this kind of PR corpo talk and actually revealing their true personality and their true motivations."

Buteau emphasis: owning relationships directly — events, texting key contacts — not just tweeting.
