# Key extracted facts

## Goal

> "a playground for building groundbreaking infrastructure concepts"

Theme: "Programmable Trust." Frame: open exploration of ideas at any depth/breadth, with EigenLayer team collaboration.

## Target candidates

Selection criteria:
- Self-motivation — fellow owns development and implementation of their own ideas
- Strong technical foundation, ability to dive deep into a specific domain
- Comfort with public-facing work + accepting criticism
- Coding competence ("use code as a tool to communicate with computers")
- Prior crypto app or systems-design experience preferred
- "Love of open innovation"

## Application process

- Rolling applications via bit.ly/eigenlayerrf
- Priority before October 27, 2023
- Two interview rounds
- Optional Devconnect Hacker House (expenses covered)

## Program phases (Season 1)

| Phase | Period | Activity |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Oct–Nov 2023 | Selection interviews + optional Devconnect networking |
| 1 — Ideation | Dec 2023 | Brainstorm project ideas with EigenLayer core team |
| 2 — Build | Dec 2023 – Jan 2024 | Design / build / iterate with engineering support |
| 3 — Present | Feb 2024 | Present project to wider peer group |

## Cohort + commitment

- Maximum 5 fellows for Season 1
- ~10 hours/week for 3 months (program runs ~2.5 months: Dec 11, 2023 – Feb 16, 2024)
- Remote (Telegram + Google Meet) or in-person

## Support cadence

- Weekly group calls
- Individual meetings
- Monthly demos
- "consistent research and engineering support from the core EigenLayer Research Team"

## Outputs from fellows

- One-or-two-pager about the project
- Public presentation

## Compensation/recognition

- Swag: Nike Air Force 1s, sweater, t-shirt, hats
- Travel: round-trip flight + accommodation for Devconnect (Istanbul)
- In-person coworking in NYC and Seattle
- Potential direct grant funding ("teams or individuals as appropriate")
- Recognition as "official part of the EigenLayer protocol"

## Framing language

Emphasis on "wider group of peers" and "open innovation" — research-community framing without explicit "academic peers" terminology. Implicit positioning: peer-of-EigenLayer-research-team, not student-of.
