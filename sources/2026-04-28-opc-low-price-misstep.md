# Source: Low Pricing — One-Person Company's Wrong Turn

Xiaohongshu post arguing that low pricing is a systematic failure mode for 一人公司 (one-person companies), with Alex Hormozi's 3-tier product structure as the counter-framework.

## The failure pattern

"写了20篇开始燃尽，每篇触达几十个人，卖39块的东西连咖啡钱都赚不回来" — after writing 20 posts, burnout sets in; each post reaches only dozens of people; ¥39 products don't earn back even the cost of a coffee.

Structural cause: cheap products require volume → volume requires production grind → grind burns out the solo operator before volume reaches break-even.

## Hormozi's 3-tier product structure (counter-framework)

Start premium and non-scalable first → then mid-tier → then mass market. Reverses the conventional instinct to start accessible/cheap.

**Tesla as illustration:** Roadster (¥250K+) → Model S → Model 3. Each expansion increased reach while the premium tier maintained value perception for the whole line.

**Diagnostic question:** "你现在的产品结构里，有没有一个'贵10倍'的选项?" (Does your current product mix include a "10x more expensive" option?)

Mechanism: a premium 1-on-1 tier generates enough revenue per unit that the operator isn't grinding volume; it also sets the reference frame so that cheaper products later feel like *accessible access* to something valuable, not the default cheap option.
