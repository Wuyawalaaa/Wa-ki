# A Conversation with Daniela Amodei — Sixth Street "It's Not Magic"

**Speaker:** Daniela Amodei, Co-Founder and President of Anthropic
**Episode context:** SF season episode 4. Recorded January 2026.

## Topics covered

### AI capabilities & adoption
- Claude Opus 4.5 framed as a significant capability jump, particularly in coding.
- Claude Code lets developers work with the tool ~100% of the time, "roughly doubling productivity."
- Agentic abilities have crossed a meaningful threshold — Claude can autonomously manage computer files and tasks.
- Adoption rates "significantly faster than anything else" historically.

### Human-AI collaboration philosophy
- "Developers are better with Claude Code than Claude Code alone" or developers working independently.
- Vision: humans handle architecture, product vision, and customer relationships; AI handles routine coding tasks.
- "Human in the loop" framed not as removing autonomy but as ensuring models "are not veering into a direction we don't want."

### Constitutional AI & training method
- Rather than reward/punishment training, Anthropic uses a **framework approach** — training models against ethical documents (UN Declaration of Human Rights, Apple terms of service).
- Claim: this approach simultaneously increased **both ethical behavior and overall intelligence**.
- "Hold light and shade" cited as a core value — acknowledging both risks and opportunities simultaneously.

### Adoption & equity
- Early adopters predictably skew toward the resourced (MIT computer scientists, enterprises).
- AI adoption occurring at unprecedented speeds vs prior technologies.
- Anthropic explicitly works on "beneficial deployments" via Gates Foundation, Clinton Health Initiative.

### Humanities & liberal arts perspective
- Daniela studied **literature** and played **flute** in college.
- **Orchestral thinking**: views team-building like assembling an orchestra — instruments complementing each other, not just individual quality.
- Critical thinking & empathy: literature trained her to approach problems from multiple angles.
- Liberal arts hiring: Anthropic screens for **mission alignment, integrity, collaboration** — not just technical credentials.
- Advice: "It doesn't matter so much what they major in, it matters how they treat the people that they work with."

### Culture & leadership
- Scaled from 7 founders to 2,500 employees.
- "Notebooks in Slack" — leadership shares thinking openly for transparency.
- "Low politics, high integrity" — uncomfortable conversations happen directly, not behind closed doors.
- Communication style: "kind but direct" — modeled at the leadership level.

### San Francisco
- Anthropic committed to downtown SF HQ; views it as "the hometown team."
- Acknowledges tech/longtime-resident tensions; sees more mutual understanding post-pandemic.
- Frames tech companies as "good citizens."

## Notable quote

> "The way I developed judgment was getting my reps as a young person and doing the drudgery."

Followed by Daniela noting AI might let the next generation "leapfrog some of that," raising open questions about how expertise develops.
