# Verified primary content

## What the post actually documents

Anthropic Marketing's use of Claude / Claude Code across multiple marketing functions:

| Function | Claim |
|---|---|
| Influencer marketing | Scripts for influencers and podcasts → "freeing up 100+ hours per month" |
| Customer marketing | Drafts case studies in 30 minutes vs 2.5 hours → "saving 10 hours per week" |
| Digital marketing | Web development workflows → "increased team productivity by 5x year-over-year" |
| Product marketing | Skills and Projects for launch briefs → "saving 5-10 hours per launch" |
| Partner marketing | Self-serve event enablement → "reducing time spent on trade show prep by 40%" |

This is broader than the single-growth-marketer framing implied by secondary coverage.

## Austin Lau's specific workflows

**Figma plugin** — creates ad creative variations across multiple aspect ratios:
- Saves "30 minutes every time he updates a large batch of creatives"
- Development time: "about 45 minutes to an hour"

**Google Ads copy workflow** — generates responsive search ads with brainstorming + CSV export:
- "what used to take 30 minutes per ad now takes 30 seconds"

## Verbatim Austin Lau quotes

> "You don't need to know how to code. All you need to know is how to explain your challenge and what you're trying to solve in a very clear, concise manner."

> "I can actually go out and build these things. So the gap between 'I wish this existed' and 'I can actually build this myself' is actually much smaller than people realize."

> "Claude is a great brainstorming partner, but sometimes it doesn't get it right on the first try. A lot of the work I do is riffing and going back and forth to help refine the copy over time."

> "All of the copy and examples that we provide Claude were written in partnership with the product marketing and copywriting teams"

## Human vs AI division (as the post articulates)

> "Austin is evaluating each headline against what he knows resonates with Anthropic's audiences. Does the value prop land? Is the tone right?"

The principle: marketers "spend less time on repetitive execution and more time on the things that matter to their job."

Human work that doesn't transfer to Claude:
- Brand-fit judgment ("does the value prop land?")
- Tone evaluation
- Foundational quality-standard setting (copy and examples Claude is trained on)
- Iterative refinement ("riffing and going back and forth")
