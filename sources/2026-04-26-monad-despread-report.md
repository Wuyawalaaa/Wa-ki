# Key extracted facts

## Timeline (pre-launch community building)

- **Oct 2022**: Discord launched with "Pioneer Role" given to all participants — backfired, many participants stayed inactive. Lesson the team drew: role criteria needed to be stricter, broader community activities needed beyond simple tasks.
- **2023 (post DragonFly Capital $19M)**: Discord opened to public; team shifted to private Telegram chat rooms to differentiate existing from new members.
- **No ecosystem-wide pre-deposit campaign** — explicitly chose against the popular new-blockchain incentive playbook.
- **No traditional onboarding platforms** (Galxe Quest, Layer3) — chose "humorous and meme-based activities on social media" instead.

## Meme character origins

- **Purple Pepe**: Team-initiated. Adopted in early stages, spread as Telegram stickers + Twitter memes; purple became Monad's identity color.
- **Molandak, Moyaki, Chog**: Community-created, foundation embraced and incorporated into branding (guilds, merch). The report states "most of the memes that now represent Monad were created by the community rather than the foundation."

## "Intern" account model

Kevin (Twitter handle: @intern at Monad) led growth. Articulated approach: "10 Rules for Crypto Marketing & Community."

> "Monad's marketing strategy, led by Kevin, focused on humorously conveying the project's image through memes rather than emphasizing its technical superiority. This approach… introduced a new trend in the Web3 industry with the 'Intern' account model. In this model, the 'Intern' account operates separately from the official account on social media, using memes to communicate market conditions, project updates, and brand identity in a lighthearted and humorous manner."

Pendle adopted the same model (Pendle Intern).

## Kevin's stated rules (excerpts from "10 Rules")

- "Stop posting ads"
- "You are competing with influencers and entertaining content on twitter, not with other protocols"
- "Association > Knowledge"
- "Quality > Quantity"

## Vibes-over-specs framing

> "Similar to how Nike emphasizes concepts like freedom, boundlessness, and energy over the technical superiority of its sneakers… Monad chose to focus on the humor, freedom, and approachability of its community. Rather than prioritizing the technical superiority of its product or the scale of its investments, Monad effectively communicated its brand image through social media."

## Community as co-creator

Bill (Monad Community Lead) on what defines a community:
> "a true community must be more than just a collection of audiences… a genuine community is defined by the interconnectedness of its members — a space where people actively interact, exchange ideas, and collaboratively create new value, such as memes."

Mechanisms:
- Weekly "Memes of the Week" — foundation highlights winning memes + creators on official account.
- Community-driven outputs: video production, physical merch, SoundCloud mixtapes based on Monad memes.
- Community-organized meetups across Japan, China, Thailand, South Korea — without foundation initiation.

## Funded resources (what made this possible)

- Recruited influencers/KOLs (Kevin, Threadguy, Tunez, Bill) into formal marketing/ecosystem/growth roles.
- Promoted long-term active community members into community-manager roles per country.
- $19M Series A from DragonFly Capital (2023) provided the budget for paid community staff.

These distinguish Monad's structure from any solo-founder copy of the playbook.
