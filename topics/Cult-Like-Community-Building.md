# Cult-Like Community Building

> A pre-launch / pre-product community-building pattern that rallies people around shared identity, vibes, and inside-culture rather than technical specifications — most cleanly executed by Monad before mainnet.

## Pattern definition

【推断】The pattern: build cohesion around culture, not features. People rally around vibes and shared identity, not technical specifications. The brand sells what the audience is *about*, not what the product *does* — the same posture by which Nike sells freedom rather than shoes, or Red Bull sells "the spirit of challenge" rather than caffeine content. [CI]

## The Monad case

### Timeline

【事实】[DS]

| Period | Move |
|---|---|
| Oct 2022 | Discord launched. All participants given a "Pioneer Role" — backfired (many stayed inactive) |
| 2023 (post DragonFly $19M Series A) | Discord opened to public; team shifted to private Telegram chat rooms to differentiate existing from new members |
| Throughout pre-launch | Explicitly chose against ecosystem-wide pre-deposit campaigns and traditional onboarding platforms (Galxe Quest, Layer3); chose meme-driven social activity instead |

### Three load-bearing mechanisms

#### 1. Meme identity before product

【事实】Purple Pepe was team-initiated and spread as Telegram stickers + Twitter memes; purple became Monad's identity color. Subsequent characters — Molandak, Moyaki, Chog — were community-created, then formally embraced by the foundation through guilds and merchandise. "Most of the memes that now represent Monad were created by the community rather than the foundation." [DS]

#### 2. The "Intern" account model

【事实】Kevin (handle: @intern at Monad) led growth and operated a separate persona-account from the official Monad handle. The Intern account uses memes to communicate updates / market commentary / brand identity in a lighthearted register; the official account stays formal. [DS]

> "Monad's marketing strategy, led by Kevin, focused on humorously conveying the project's image through memes rather than emphasizing its technical superiority. This approach… introduced a new trend in the Web3 industry with the 'Intern' account model. In this model, the 'Intern' account operates separately from the official account on social media, using memes to communicate market conditions, project updates, and brand identity in a lighthearted and humorous manner." [DS]

【事实】The pattern was copied by other projects (Pendle Intern is the named example). [DS]

【事实】Kevin's "10 Rules for Crypto Marketing & Community" articulates the posture: "Stop posting ads"; "You are competing with influencers and entertaining content on twitter, not with other protocols"; "Association > Knowledge"; "Quality > Quantity." [DS]

#### 3. Community as co-creator

【事实】Bill, Monad's Community Lead, defined community as "more than just a collection of audiences… a genuine community is defined by the interconnectedness of its members — a space where people actively interact, exchange ideas, and collaboratively create new value, such as memes." [DS]

Manifestations: [DS]
- Weekly "Memes of the Week" — foundation highlights winning memes + creators on the official account
- Community-driven outputs beyond memes: video production, physical merchandise, SoundCloud mixtapes
- Self-organized meetups in Japan, China, Thailand, South Korea — without foundation initiation

## Vibes-over-specs framing

【事实】[DS]

> "Similar to how Nike emphasizes concepts like freedom, boundlessness, and energy over the technical superiority of its sneakers… Monad chose to focus on the humor, freedom, and approachability of its community. Rather than prioritizing the technical superiority of its product or the scale of its investments, Monad effectively communicated its brand image through social media."

## Long cult vs. short cult

【推断】The cautionary half of the pattern: communities built on incentives (airdrops, speculation) collapse when incentives end. Communities built on shared *intellectual* identity persist past any specific campaign. The asset that compounds is belief in a frame, not exposure to a payout. Token-driven engagement is a short cult; idea-driven engagement is a long one. [CI]

## Preconditions for replication

【事实】Monad was not a solo-founder project at the point this strategy ran — it had: [DS]
- ~$19M Series A (DragonFly Capital, 2023) funding paid community managers
- Recruited influencers / KOLs (Kevin, Threadguy, Tunez, Bill) into formal marketing / ecosystem / growth roles
- Promoted long-term active community members into per-country community-manager roles

【推断】So the structural requirements (paid community managers, recruited KOLs in formal roles) cannot be copied at zero budget. What *can* be copied: the principles — meme-before-spec, separate humorous handle from official handle, formally credit community contributions, founder personally present alongside members. The principles work without the budget; the structure does not.

【推断】The "Intern" handle is the cheapest-to-replicate piece — a second account with no infrastructure cost. But it requires a community manager (or founder) with native meme fluency, which is itself a non-trivial constraint.
