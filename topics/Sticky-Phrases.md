# Sticky Phrases

> Lulu Cheng Meservey's framework on how to engineer language that spreads — through analogy, anecdote, and rhythm — so the listener remembers it and repeats it without effort.

## Core insight

【推断】Ideas spread when they can be compressed into memorable phrases. Mitt Romney's "binders full of women," "drinking from a firehose" — language that's vivid and quotable spreads on its own. Whether a phrase becomes sticky is partly engineerable, not luck. [CI, LM]

## The three techniques

### 1. Analogy — "put the pill in some cheese"

【事实】Lulu's example. Wrap an unfamiliar / hard-to-swallow idea inside a familiar object the listener already accepts. The hidden idea rides along. [AB]

【推断】The analogy itself models the technique — "put the pill in some cheese" *is* an analogy describing analogy. Self-demonstration is one of the strongest forms of the technique.

### 2. Anecdotes over adjectives

【事实】Use stories instead of subjective descriptors. "Innovative," "passionate," "world-class" — adjective filler. [AB]

【推断】The mechanism: an adjective demands the listener trust the speaker's evaluation. An anecdote lets the listener evaluate the same thing themselves. The second mode produces stronger conviction because the listener feels they reached the conclusion independently — even when the anecdote was carefully selected to lead them there.

### 3. ABBA structure for slogans

【事实】Lulu references this structure. [LM]

【推断】The ABBA pattern — "Ask not what your country can do for you, ask what you can do for your country" — has the rhetorical move: state A then B, then mirror back B then A. The chiasmus creates rhythm and a sense of resolution that the brain registers as wisdom. JFK's line is the canonical example; the structure is reusable.

## How to test stickiness

【推断】The test isn't "does this phrase sound good when I say it?" — it's "does someone else use this phrase without my prompting?" Phrases that work spread on their own; phrases that don't, drop. The discipline is to engineer many, ship them, and observe which ones get repeated by others. [CI]

## Engineering vs. luck

【推断】The framework rejects the romantic view that quotable phrases are accidents. They can be engineered — using the techniques above — and the engineering is iterative: try ten framings, ship them in real content, watch which get quoted back. The ones repeated by others are the ones to keep. [CI]

## Why this matters strategically

【推断】Memorable phrases are a category of [[Generative-Engine-Optimization]] avant-la-lettre — phrases that get repeated also get indexed, both by humans and by LLM training corpora. A phrase that becomes the canonical way to refer to a concept becomes the answer when someone searches the concept. The first sticky phrase tends to win; later phrases for the same concept are uphill battles.

## Failure modes

【推断】**Force-fitting the structure.** ABBA chiasmus on every line collapses into clever-for-clever's-sake and the listener tunes out. The structure works because it's used sparingly — high signal at the right moment, not constant.

【推断】**Engineered-sounding analogies.** "Put the pill in some cheese" works partly *because* it doesn't sound polished — it sounds like an actual person reaching for a comparison. Over-polishing kills the warmth and the listener registers manipulation.

【推断】**Self-flattering phrasing.** Sticky phrases describing your own product / company tend not to spread, because the listener's brain tags them as marketing copy and rejects them. Sticky phrases describing the *world* — the problem, the absurdity, the gap — spread, because anyone in the world can repeat them without flagging themselves as a salesman.
