# Minto Pyramid Principle

> Barbara Minto's communication framework: lead with the conclusion, then support it with logically grouped, MECE-structured reasoning. Originally developed at McKinsey for management-consulting deliverables; widely adapted for any high-stakes communication.

## Core structure

【事实】Two essential moves: [MS]

1. **Lead with the conclusion** — state the main point in a single sentence at the top.
2. **Support with reasoning** — explain why; supply evidence and examples beneath the conclusion.

## What the structure forces

【事实】[MS]

- Get to the core point immediately, with no preamble — "立刻进入核心观点，避免绕半天还没说重点."
- Establish a clear position rather than generalized commentary.
- Supply substantive evidence rather than unsupported claims.

## MECE — the supporting-evidence rule

【事实】Supporting points must be **Mutually Exclusive** (non-overlapping) and **Collectively Exhaustive** (comprehensively cover the thesis without redundancy). [MS]

【推断】MECE is the load-bearing constraint that distinguishes Minto from generic "TL;DR first" advice. Without MECE, conclusion-first writing devolves into a single point repeated three times. With MECE, the supporting layer earns its weight by partitioning the argument cleanly.

## Why it travels well

【推断】The Pyramid Principle outlasted its consulting origin because it answers a problem that exists wherever a reader's attention is scarce: who reads the whole thing? In long-form, the reader who quits mid-way still got the conclusion. In short-form, the same logic explains why social-media content benefits from it (see [[Minto-This-Skill]]).
