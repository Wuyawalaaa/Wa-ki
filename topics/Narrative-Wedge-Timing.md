# Narrative Wedge Timing

> The framework that timing the narrative wedge matters as much as the wedge itself. Plant a category term too early, nobody cares. Plant it too late, someone else owns it. The window is wider than it feels but not infinite.

## Source pattern

【事实】Joel John's "Narrative Wedge" essay (Decentralised.co, 2023-08-03): [DC]

> "Narratives pick up steam at the beginning of a bull market. These tailwinds usually occur due to technological developments."

Attention precedes capital — new technologies (DeFi smart contracts, NFT standards, play-to-earn mechanics) generate excitement → drives prices upward → attracts venture capital. Self-reinforcing during bull markets, collapses when narratives exhaust themselves.

## What the wedge actually is

【事实】The wedge in Joel John's framing is the **timing mismatch between narrative cycles and product development cycles**: [DC]

| Cycle | Duration |
|---|---|
| Narrative | Months — ramps fast, exhausts faster |
| Product development | 18–24+ months |

A founder who picks a theme during a narrative peak ships into a dead narrative. The wedge widens during the build, and when the product finally launches, the attention has already moved on.

> 【事实】"Themes picked by founders and investors based on narrative windfalls can be terrible as attention flows elsewhere by the time products go live." [DC]

## Worked example: Axie Infinity (Joel John's case)

【事实】Token prices peaked October 2021 while user adoption had already plateaued. Venture-backed Web3 gaming startups launched January 2022 onward — by then users had vanished and venture funding dried up. The product timeline (multi-year build) and narrative timeline (peaked Oct '21) were misaligned. [DC]

## When narrative + product align

【事实】The exception to wedge collapse: [DC]

> "Compound, UniSwap and Bored Apes are each an instance of narrative tailwinds blending with product usage to produce outsized returns for investors."

These projects entered during narrative peaks while simultaneously gaining authentic user adoption. The timing aligned: narrative attention matched product-market fit maturation.

## Two distinct traps Joel John names

【事实】[DC]

| Trap | What goes wrong |
|---|---|
| Too early | "Being early is a moat in itself. But it could also mean it takes months before someone builds conviction on what you are making." |
| Too late | "If one is to go by price action alone as a driver for emerging theses, the odds are pretty high that they are entering a market very late." |

The ultimate risk: launching products into dead narratives — the launch becomes "a bet on the return of a bull market." [DC]

## Application to category-term planting

【推断】A specific high-leverage application of the wedge framework: timing **when** you plant a coined category term ([[Category-Design]]). [CI]

```
TOO EARLY: term planted before attention exists in the space
    → no one is searching for the topic
        → term doesn't propagate
            → sticks only with the originator's audience
                → window closes; term forgotten

JUST RIGHT: term planted at the start of attention rise
    → attention rises with the term in it
        → propagation accelerates with attention growth
            → term becomes the canonical reference
                → originator becomes implicit canonical source

TOO LATE: term planted after attention is established
    → competing terms already in discourse
        → forced to displace existing vocabulary
            → almost impossible to unseat established term
                → category leadership has been claimed
```

The just-right window is wider than founders typically feel but is bounded on both sides.

## Reading the cycle

【推断】Three signals to watch for the start-of-cycle inflection:

1. **Adjacent-sector attention rise.** When attention starts moving from one sector into a related one, that's the early phase of the new sector's cycle.
2. **First articulate critique of current paradigm.** When public discourse starts naming what's wrong with the dominant existing approach, the new alternative's window has opened.
3. **Multiple independent practitioners reaching for shared vocabulary.** When you see different people independently inventing similar terms for the same emerging concept, the window is wide open and *will close fast* — vocabulary lock-in is days, not months, away once the convergence starts.

## The "wider than it feels" qualifier

【推断】Founders intuiting the wedge often feel they've missed the window when they actually haven't. Two reasons the perception is wrong:

1. **Self-attention bias.** The founder has been thinking about the topic for months / years; their internal model treats the topic as "old news." But the broader audience is only just discovering it. The founder feels late; the audience experiences early.
2. **Attention cycles aren't sharp edges.** Narrative cycles ramp over weeks-to-months, not overnight. The "ramp-up" phase is the entire window for category planting — and it's longer than it feels in real time.

## When the window has actually closed

【推断】Concrete signals you're past the window:

- A competing term has been in discourse for >6 months
- AI engines, when asked about the topic, return the competing term (not yours)
- Other practitioners cite a paper / post that named the competing term first
- The competing term has appeared in Series A / B announcements as positioning

If two or more of these hold, attempting to displace the term is a long bet against compounding momentum. Better to either pick a related-but-distinct sub-term, or accept and use the existing vocabulary while differentiating on substance.

## Active monitoring discipline

【推断】If you're attempting [[Category-Design]] in a domain, the wedge framework implies an ongoing scanning practice: [CI]

- Monitor for any competing terms emerging in adjacent project announcements
- React fast if someone else seeds adjacent vocabulary — either fork your term to differentiate, or absorb the competing term into yours
- Don't wait for "the perfect time" — attention dynamics don't reward patience past the inflection point

## Failure modes

【推断】**Waiting for the thesis / paper / product to be ready before seeding the term.** This is the most common failure mode for technically-substantive founders — they want the rigor of formal definition before public use. But the cycle won't wait; competitors will name the space first. The principle: start using your term provisionally, even before formal definition exists.

【推断】**Treating "early" as safe.** Planting a term too early before attention exists at all produces zero compounding — the term sits in your content but nobody else uses it because nobody else is talking about the topic. "Early" is only a tailwind during the attention ramp, not before it.

【推断】**Misreading a cycle as still-ramping when it's already mid.** Cycles can look like they're still building when they're actually peaking. By the time mainstream coverage names a sector, the wedge window has typically closed. Mainstream coverage = lagging indicator.
