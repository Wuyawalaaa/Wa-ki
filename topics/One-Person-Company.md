# One-Person Company

> A solo operation that uses leverage (audience, standardized products, automation) to reach scale that would traditionally require a team. The replicable case study most often cited is Justin Welsh.

## Case: Justin Welsh

【事实】Former senior VP at a large tech company; left due to burnout and built a solo operation reportedly generating **$5M+ annually with 90%+ profit margins**. [WH]

【推断】The dollar figures are repeated across many Chinese-language summaries of Welsh and originate in his own marketing copy and interview soundbites. Directional rather than independently audited — useful as a "scale is possible" reference point, not as a precise benchmark.

## Three replicable steps (WaveHeart's distillation)

【事实】[WH]

| # | Step | Chinese | What it covers |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Establish professional credibility | 建立专业标签 | Personal brand / topical authority |
| 2 | Create standardized products | 做标准化产品 | Reproducible deliverables (courses, templates, cohorts) |
| 3 | Build automated systems | 搭自动化系统 | Marketing, fulfillment, payments running without operator presence |

【推断】The step list is the WaveHeart paraphrase, not Welsh's exact taxonomy. Welsh himself names slightly different phases in his own writing — but the three-step shape (audience → product → systems) is the load-bearing pattern across most one-person-company case writeups.

## Stated principle

【事实】**Leverage** is the named operating principle: it allows one person to reach global markets without hiring employees. [WH]

## Where the leverage actually compounds

【推断】The three steps aren't independent — they're **sequential leverage stages**, each multiplying the prior:

| Stage | What it leverages | Without it, you have |
|---|---|---|
| Credibility | Attention scarcity → audience | Skills with no buyers |
| Standardized products | Personal time → repeatable units | An audience you can only sell consulting hours to |
| Automation | Operator presence → 24/7 fulfillment | Standardized products you still have to manually deliver |

Skip stage 1 and stages 2–3 have no demand. Skip stage 2 and stage 3 has nothing to automate. The framework holds because each layer's output is the prior layer's input.

## Why "super individual" is more than a marketing term

【推断】The Chinese term **超级个体 (super individual)** is doing real work the English phrase "solopreneur" doesn't. *Solopreneur* implies a category of small business; *super individual* implies a person whose individual leverage now matches what previously required a firm. The framing matters because it changes the comparison set: a solopreneur compares to other solopreneurs and feels small; a super individual compares to mid-sized firms and asks "what does the firm have that I don't, and can I substitute it with leverage?" The reframe is the prerequisite for the strategy to feel reasonable rather than fringe.
