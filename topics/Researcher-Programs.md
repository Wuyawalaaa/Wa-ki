# Researcher Programs

> A formal cohort-based program that turns external researchers into peer collaborators on a project's open problems — building both peer-reviewed credibility and a community of practice around the project's ideas before launch.

## Pattern definition

【推断】The pattern: rather than producing research alone and seeking external validation later, run a structured program that brings outside researchers *inside* the work — co-designing, peer-reviewing, and presenting alongside the core team. The output is twofold: better research, and a community of academic peers personally invested in the project's success. The community is the second product. [CI]

## Case: EigenLayer Research Fellowship (Season 1, 2023–2024)

### Goal as stated

【事实】"a playground for building groundbreaking infrastructure concepts" themed on "Programmable Trust." [ER]

### Structure

【事实】[ER]

| Phase | Period | Activity |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Oct–Nov 2023 | Selection interviews + optional Devconnect networking |
| 1 — Ideation | Dec 2023 | Brainstorm project ideas with core team |
| 2 — Build | Dec 2023 – Jan 2024 | Design / build / iterate with hands-on engineering support |
| 3 — Present | Feb 2024 | Project presentation to wider peer group |

### Cohort + cadence

【事实】[ER]
- Maximum 5 fellows for Season 1
- ~10 hours/week for 3 months (program ran Dec 11, 2023 – Feb 16, 2024)
- Remote (Telegram + Google Meet) or in-person (NYC and Seattle coworking)
- Weekly group calls + individual meetings + monthly demos
- "consistent research and engineering support from the core EigenLayer Research Team"

### Selection criteria

【事实】Self-motivation, strong technical foundation, comfort with public-facing work, coding competence, prior crypto app or systems-design experience, "love of open innovation." [ER]

### What fellows produce

【事实】A one-or-two-pager about the project + public presentation. [ER]

### What fellows receive

【事实】[ER]
- Swag (Nike Air Force 1s, sweater, t-shirt, hats)
- Travel (round-trip flight + accommodation for Devconnect Istanbul)
- In-person coworking access
- Potential direct grant funding
- Recognition as "official part of the EigenLayer protocol"

### Framing language

【事实】Emphasis on "wider group of peers" and "open innovation" — peer-of-research-team framing rather than student-of. [ER]

## Why the pattern works

【推断】Three compounding effects: [CI]

1. **Peer review as structural input.** Fellows critique each other's and the core team's work. This produces internal pressure for higher rigor than any solo research effort.
2. **Discourse leadership.** Five published fellows + core team = ~6–10 voices producing substantive content on the project's themes. Over time this volume of associated discourse becomes hard to ignore in the surrounding research conversation.
3. **Personal investment.** Fellows whose names appear in the program's outputs have reputational stakes in the project's success. This converts external researchers into informal advocates.

## Solo-founder / zero-budget decomposition

【推断】The Eigenlayer structure is expensive (travel, swag, coworking, grants). The principle decomposes into lighter-weight equivalents that capture most of the value: [CI]

| EigenLayer component | Lighter-weight version |
|---|---|
| Formal research fellowship | Reading group: invite 5–10 researchers to discuss a paper draft. Free. Weekly call. |
| Weekly calls with core team | Office hours: weekly X Spaces / Zoom on open mechanism-design problems |
| Fellows produce research | Open-question framing: "here's an unsolved problem in [domain] — who wants to take a crack?" |
| Swag + travel stipends | Recognition: co-author opportunities, public credit, citation in published work |
| Selective application process | Soft invitation: DMs to specific researchers, no formal application |

【推断】The trade-off: lighter versions lose the *commitment* effect of a formal program (the swag and structure are partly there to make participation feel real). Reading groups have higher dropout and weaker reciprocity — fellows of a real program show up partly because they've publicly accepted; reading-group attendees haven't.

## Risks

【推断】**Premature institutionalization.** Running a "fellowship" before the project has the credibility to attract real researchers reads as cosplay and burns more reputation than it builds. The pattern requires a base of standing — the project has earned the right to gather researchers. Without that, the call goes unanswered and the empty program is a public failure. [CI]

【推断】The progression from independent presence to formal program is the [[Credibility-Ladder]] — researcher programs sit at the top rung, not the bottom.
