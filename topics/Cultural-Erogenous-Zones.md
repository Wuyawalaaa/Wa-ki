# Cultural Erogenous Zones

> Lulu Cheng Meservey's framework: instead of trying to change what your audience cares about, shape your message to fit into what they already care about.

## Origin

【事实】Coined by Lulu Cheng Meservey (former VP Comms at Substack, EVP Corporate Affairs at Activision Blizzard, founder of Rostra). [LM]

## Core principle

【事实】Direct quote from Lulu (via Buteau synthesis): [AB]

> "People have things that they either care about or don't and you're not going to change that. So it's a huge lift to try to change someone's worldview or their passions. It's a light lift to take the thing you want to talk about and just shape it into to fit into their worldview or their passions."

【推断】Buteau's framing: build "an API to your audience" by bridging what they already care about to your message. [AB]

## The asymmetry the framework exploits

【推断】Two operations are not equal-cost: [CI]

| Operation | Cost | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Change what the audience cares about | Heavy lift — months/years of campaigns, often fails | Rarely the right move |
| Reshape your message to land in their existing care zone | Light lift — a wording change | Default move |

The framework is essentially: don't fight the audience's settled preferences; meet them where the preferences already are.

## Mechanism

【推断】The "erogenous zone" metaphor is doing real work — it implies the audience has *pre-existing sensitivities*, places where the right framing produces immediate response while adjacent framings produce nothing. The strategist's job is reconnaissance: identify what the target audience is already animated by, then pose your idea as already living in that domain.

【推断】This is structurally identical to fitting a message to an existing schema rather than asking the listener to construct a new one. New schemas take real cognitive work; recognizing a familiar one takes none.

## Application pattern

【推断】Three steps: [CI]

1. **Reconnaissance.** What does the target audience already care about, talk about, fight about?
2. **Positioning.** Identify which of *your* concerns overlap with theirs — find the bridge.
3. **Framing.** Lead with the overlap, not with your concern in isolation.

## Failure mode

【推断】The most common error is a founder leading with what *they* find most exciting about their own work, rather than what the audience finds exciting about anything. The result reads as monologue, not conversation, and the audience tunes out before the message lands.

## Relation to neighboring frameworks

【推断】Cultural Erogenous Zones is the *messaging* layer; [[Concentric-Circles]] is the *targeting* layer. They compose: pick the audience whose existing care-zones overlap most with your message (Concentric Circles), then frame your message inside that overlap (Erogenous Zones).
