# Jenny Hoyos

> Short-form YouTube creator (~3M subscribers, ~10M views/Short avg) whose hook-and-retention formula is widely cited as a model for the underlying logic of viral short-form video.

## The hook formula (3 seconds, often less)

【事实】The hook is no more than 3 seconds, ideally less. Jenny says: [YT]

> "I really do think you have one second to hook someone, especially on Shorts."

【事实】The hook must be **concise and visually pleasing**. Power words she uses: **"banned," "free," "one dollar," "secret," "cheap"**. Example hook: **"$1 chicken sandwich"**. [ME]

【事实】The hook is so important Jenny will change the *entire video idea* if needed to make the hook strong. [ME]

【事实】The widely-cited three-step framing of her hook is **shock, intrigue, satisfy** — described this way in YouTube's official blog as a summary of her method (paraphrase, not Jenny's own naming). [YT]

## The retention structure

【事实】[ME]

- **Target retention benchmark: 90% or higher** — this is the bar she treats as required for viral reach.
- **Structure**: hook → foreshadowing → transitions → but/then storytelling → emotional close.

### Foreshadowing (immediately post-hook)

【事实】Right after the hook, signal the conflict and the expected outcome. Example: *"Chick-fil-A has the best chicken sandwiches, but I am not paying $6..."* [ME]

The foreshadow does three jobs at once: signals what's coming, surfaces the conflict, implies the expected outcome (which the video may then twist).

### Transitions

【事实】Move the story forward fast. Example: *"Let's get cooking."* [ME]

### But/Then pattern

【事实】Used to set up turns and surprises. Example: *"But is that enough to beat Chick-fil-A?"* [ME]

### Emotional close

【事实】End on highest emotional intensity — punchline, heartwarming moment, or twist. Goal: *"Leave them satisfied, but surprised."* [ME]

## The audience-first reframe

【事实】The principle the original Chinese-language source surfaced: [JH]

> 传播的关键不是"我想表达什么"，而是"用户能接受什么"
> The key to going viral is not "what I want to express," but "what the audience can absorb."

【事实】The three named capabilities the Chinese source identifies: simplify information (简化信息), create turns / pattern interrupts (制造转折), transfer emotion (传递情绪). [JH]

【推断】These three capabilities map cleanly onto the formula extracted from English sources:
- **Simplify information** → the hook-as-foreshadow compresses the entire premise into seconds.
- **Create turns** → the but/then pattern is the named technique for this.
- **Transfer emotion** → the emotional close is the named technique for this.

The Chinese source named the capabilities; the English sources name the techniques that operationalize them.

## Thumbnails — Shorts vs. channel

【事实】[YT]

> "Thumbnails are not important because 99.9% of the views are coming from the Shorts feed… As far as channel marketing and branding, I think thumbnails are very important."

The distinction: Shorts feed traffic doesn't see thumbnails (the feed plays inline). Channel-level identity does. Spend thumbnail effort on branding, not on Shorts hooks.

## Why this travels beyond YouTube

【推断】The substantive insight — *the creator's job is not to express well, it is to be receivable* — is structurally the same move as Nielsen's [[Articulation-Barrier]] in AI UX. Both observe that under attention scarcity, the user's tolerance for "you express, I decode" collapses. Hoyos's three-second hook is the short-form-video answer to the same constraint Nielsen's multimodal Intent Surface is the AI-UX answer to. Different domains, same cognitive economics.

【推断】The 90% retention benchmark is unusually clean as a forcing function. Most creator advice is hand-wavy on what "engagement" means. "90% watched to the end" is a single number that any creator can measure and that the platform algorithm rewards. Compare with [[Sell-Before-Build]]'s 30% conversion rate: both are concrete numerical benchmarks that turn a fuzzy goal into a binary go/no-go signal.
