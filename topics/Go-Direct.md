# Go Direct

> The doctrine that founders should bypass traditional PR / journalist intermediaries and speak directly to their own audience on their own channels — owning narrative, voice, and timing themselves.

## Origin

【事实】Articulated as a manifesto by Lulu Cheng Meservey (Rostra) on her Flack Substack. The company name *Rostra* — after the speaker's platform in ancient Rome — is itself the metaphor: a direct platform from which a leader speaks to their audience without intermediaries. [GD]

## Three-part argument

【事实】[GD]

1. **Traditional PR is obsolete.** Media gatekeepers and third-party publicists are no longer necessary given direct social/email access to audiences.
2. **Founders must own communications.** "The best spokesperson for any endeavor is… the person who holds the secret knowledge" — cannot be outsourced to hired representatives.
3. **Direct narrative building.** Founders should "craft and tell your own story, without being dependent on intermediaries" to maintain control and competitive advantage.

## Core principle

【事实】Direct quote from Lulu (via Buteau): [AB]

> "The crux of what it means is for the founder and the originator of the project to speak directly to the audience without middlemen, without screens, without filtering it through all this kind of PR corpo talk and actually revealing their true personality and their true motivations."

## What "direct" actually means

【推断】Three layers, in increasing depth: [AB]

| Layer | What it is |
|---|---|
| Tweet / post | Default surface. Necessary but not sufficient. |
| Owned long-form (Substack, Mirror, blog) | Where positions get fully argued |
| Direct relationships | Texting key contacts, hosting events, in-person — owning the relationship, not just the broadcast |

Buteau's emphasis: the deeper layers are where Go Direct is actually executed. Founders who only tweet have stopped at the shallowest surface and miss most of the doctrine.

## The institutional version

【推断】a16z's New Media operation is Go Direct executed at venture-firm scale: a daily podcast, a daily newsletter, an 8-week New Media Fellowship to systematize storyteller training. When a16z announces a fund or thesis, they don't need a journalist to validate it — they have their own distribution. [AZ]

【推断】The fact that a major institutional investor adopted Go Direct as standard operating procedure is evidence the doctrine isn't just for solo founders without access to PR — it's a strategic choice even where access is unlimited.

- **Targeting layer**: [[Concentric-Circles]] — Go Direct says "speak directly," Concentric Circles says "directly to whom, in what order"
- **Messaging layer**: [[Cultural-Erogenous-Zones]] — once you've targeted, fit your message to their existing concerns
- **Tactical layer**: [[Sticky-Phrases]] + [[Physics-Based-Communications]] — the actual mechanics of what to say and how to deploy force
- **Vehicle layer**: [[Founder-As-KOL]] — the practice form Go Direct usually takes (founder personal account as primary distribution surface)

## Failure mode

【推断】"Go Direct" is sometimes mistaken for "post a lot." But posting frequency without the underlying willingness to argue real positions, take real stances, reveal actual motivations — produces output indistinguishable from corporate-handle filler. The doctrine's load-bearing claim is *direct* in the sense of unmediated and personal, not in the sense of high-volume.
