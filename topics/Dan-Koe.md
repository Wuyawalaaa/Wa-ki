# Dan Koe

> Million-follower creator whose framework reframes content creation as a systematized 2-hour daily process rather than an inspiration-driven craft. Repeatedly cited as the canonical reference for solo-founder content production.

## Core framework: 2-hour daily SOP

【事实】[XD]

| # | Principle | Mechanic |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Test first | 280-character posts validate topic reception before expanding to long-form |
| 2 | AI-powered research | AI summarizes source material and surfaces new connections — compresses ~6h of research into ~1h |
| 3 | Content repurposing | One core piece → 7–8 platform variants (do not author each separately) |
| 4 | Daily focus | Just 2 outputs/day: one newsletter paragraph + 3 social posts |
| 5 | Template-based creation | AI deconstructs viral structures; **30% proven-successful / 70% experimental** content ratio |

## Diagnosed inefficiency sources

【事实】Three failure modes the framework targets: repetitive work, underutilized AI tools, and platform silos. [XD]

## Stated principle

【事实】"Content creation can be systematized and streamlined through repeatable processes rather than relying on inspiration." [XD]

## Why the test-first step is the load-bearing one

【推断】The other four principles are efficiency moves; **test-first** is the strategic move. A 280-char post is cheap enough that a creator can run dozens per week and read the response signal before spending the long-form research budget. This inverts the default failure pattern in solo content (write the long piece, hope it lands, learn nothing if it doesn't). The mechanism is the same one [[Sell-Before-Build]] uses for products: validate cheap, then commit. The 30/70 ratio in principle 5 is the same idea applied to format — re-run what's already proven, allocate the experimental budget consciously.

【推断】The 6h→1h research compression and the 30/70 ratio are paraphrased numbers from a Chinese summary, not Dan's primary source. Treat as directional rather than precise.
