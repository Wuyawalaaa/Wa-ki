# Staged Launch Sequence

> A product launch is not a single post but a multi-act sequence (demo → launch → behind-the-scenes → tutorial). Used by mori尧 to turn a two-digit-follower X account into 30k views, 200+ users, and 1,043 messages over 3 days.

## Reported outcome

【事实】mori尧 launched a product on X (Twitter) with a **two-digit follower count initially**. Three-day metrics: **30k views, 200+ users, 1,043 messages**. [MO]

【推断】Self-reported metrics from a single creator. Directional rather than benchmark — useful as evidence the small-follower-count launch is *possible*, not as a target conversion ratio.

## Four stated strategies

【事实】[MO]

| # | Strategy | Mechanic |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Precise targeting** | Identify the desired audience segment *before* crafting the narrative (mori targeted creatives interested in design, products, AI) |
| 2 | **Staged storytelling** | Multiple posts from different angles: demo → formal launch → behind-the-scenes video → tutorial |
| 3 | **Audience-centric messaging** | The intersection of "what you want to share" and "what your audience cares about" — not what you want to say |
| 4 | **Video content** | Screen recordings or talking-head — "X is full of nerds" who rarely see coherent spoken content |

## Stated closing reflection

【事实】"In the AI era, **novelty and personal resonance** matter more than mere utility in software products." [MO]

## Why staged storytelling is the load-bearing one

【推断】Strategies 1, 3, 4 are well-known (target audience, audience-first framing, use video). The genuinely distinctive contribution is strategy 2: **a launch is a sequence, not a post**.

The conventional small-creator launch model is one big post (the "Show HN" / Product Hunt single-shot moment). Strategies 2 inverts this — instead of one post that has to do everything, four posts each doing one job:

| Post | Job |
|---|---|
| Demo | Curiosity hook — "this is interesting" |
| Formal launch | Conversion CTA — "you can use this now" |
| Behind-the-scenes video | Trust signal — "real human, real work" |
| Tutorial | Activation enabler — "here's how to actually use it" |

Each post recruits a different cohort: scrollers (demo), already-warm fans (launch), skeptics (BTS), users on the fence about adoption (tutorial). One post optimized for any of these cohorts is sub-optimal for the others — separating them lets each be sharper.

## Why this works on X with small follower count

【推断】X's algorithm rewards engagement velocity, which favors *multiple* posts within a short window — each post resets the algorithmic exposure clock and broadens the audience graph. A single big launch post on a small account dies fast because there's no follower base to seed early engagement; a sequence keeps re-entering the feed and accumulates compounding signal.

【推断】The "X is full of nerds → video advantage" claim is plausible at the *small-creator scale* specifically: most small accounts post text or screenshots, so a coherent talking-head video is differentiation by absence rather than absolute quality.

## Limits and caveats

【推断】The 4 strategies are mori尧's **post-hoc retrospective** of a launch that worked — not a controlled experiment. Order may reflect storytelling, not causal weight. Treat the framework as one operator's account, not a tested protocol.

【推断】This is a **launch-window** playbook, not an ongoing-engagement strategy. It complements [[Series-Content-Strategy]] (which is about sustained recurring content) but doesn't replace it. The launch is the spike; series-content is the baseline.
