# a16z Crypto Comms Framework

> a16z crypto's structured enumeration of the communications levers available to a crypto founder, with the framing that no single lever is best — the right mix depends on stage, audience, and goal.

## The lever inventory

【事实】a16z crypto enumerates seven channels: [AZ]

| Lever | What it is |
|---|---|
| Owned content | Blogs, videos, whitepapers — first-party long-form |
| Social — brand | Corporate / project handle |
| Social — personal | Founder personal account |
| Community | Discord, Telegram, Signal — direct member relationships |
| Speaking | Conferences, IRL events |
| Media / press | Journalists, podcasts |
| KOL relationships | Influencer outreach |

## Core principle

【事实】"No single tactic is best — the right mix depends on stage, audience, and goals." [AZ]

【推断】The principle reframes comms strategy from "what should I do?" to "where am I, and which lever does *that* unlock?" — a stage-conditional question rather than a best-practice question.

## Sequencing — when each lever becomes available

【推断】Not all levers work at all stages. From the inventory's adaptation to a zero-budget zero-followers founder: [CI]

| Lever | Available at stage zero? | When it activates |
|---|---|---|
| Owned content | Yes | Always — own your blog from day one |
| Social — personal | Yes | Always — primary distribution engine |
| Social — brand | Partially — secondary, retweets founder | Activates as project gains independent identity |
| Community | No | Premature without a follower base — opening Discord at zero is empty room |
| Speaking | Spaces guest appearances only | Hosting / panels require credibility built first |
| Media / press | No | No story without traction |
| KOL outreach | Soft only — DMs to specific accounts | Formal outreach assumes you have something to offer |

Activating a later lever before its preconditions are in place produces the failure modes the framework is designed to prevent.

## Why this is a useful framework

【推断】Three things the lever inventory does:

1. **Makes choices explicit.** Founders without the inventory tend to oscillate between levers based on what's currently fashionable. The list forces the question "which of these am I actually using, and why?"
2. **Reveals gaps.** A founder running only owned content + brand-social is missing the founder-personal lever — which is usually the highest-leverage one. Gap-detection is half the value.
3. **Prevents premature activation.** Listing community, media, and KOL alongside the basics reminds operators that those *exist* without recommending them yet — a useful counterweight to the urge to "do PR."

## The a16z meta-position

【推断】a16z themselves practice an aggressive form of [[Go-Direct]]: their own daily podcast + daily newsletter + 8-week New Media Fellowship (mid-2025) systematize founder-direct comms inside the firm. When a16z announces a fund or thesis, they don't need a journalist to validate it — they have their own distribution. [AZ]

【推断】The fact that a major institutional investor adopted Go Direct as standard operating procedure is evidence the doctrine isn't just for founders without media access — it's a strategic choice even where access is unlimited. The lever framework's "no single tactic is best" framing notwithstanding, a16z's revealed preference is heavy weight on owned + social, light weight on traditional press.

## Failure modes

【推断】**Treating the list as a checklist.** A founder activating all seven levers simultaneously is doing none of them well — each requires its own discipline and audience-cultivation work. The list is a menu, not an instruction.

【推断】**Stage-misjudgment.** The most common error is overestimating current stage and activating a lever the project hasn't earned. Opening a Discord at zero followers is the canonical example — empty community channels produce reverse social proof.
