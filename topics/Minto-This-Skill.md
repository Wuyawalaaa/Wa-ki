# /Minto This Skill

> Ole Lehmann's adaptation of the [[Minto-Pyramid-Principle]] into a slash-invokable LLM skill for social-media content. Forces conclusion-first, MECE-structured posts.

## What it does

【事实】Restructures a piece of content so it: [MS]

1. **Leads with the conclusion** — single-sentence main point at the top.
2. **Supports with MECE-structured reasoning** — non-overlapping, comprehensive evidence and examples beneath.

## What it is built on

【事实】Directly derived from Barbara Minto's [[Minto-Pyramid-Principle]] — originally a McKinsey-era management-consulting communication framework. [MS]

## Application surfaces

【事实】小红书 posts, public accounts (公众号), tweets, video scripts, and general communication clarity. [MS]

## Demonstrated results

【事实】Ole Lehmann reportedly produces 10M+ monthly impressions and $5,000+/month income on X using this method consistently. [MS]

【推断】The numbers do real work in two directions. They establish that the framework survives the jump from long-form consulting decks to short-form social media (the original Minto context vs. the new one). And they hint at a leverage ratio — one structural skill, applied at scale across content production, produces compounding distribution. The cost of running the skill is near-zero per post; the upside accumulates.

## Critical read

【推断】"Lead with conclusion" alone is generic advice; the load-bearing piece of /Minto this — and the reason Lehmann's version works — is the **MECE enforcement** on supporting points. Conclusion-first writing without MECE collapses into "say the thing, then say it again three times." MECE is what keeps the pyramid honest. Anyone copying this skill should test specifically that MECE shows up in the output, not just the conclusion-first ordering.
